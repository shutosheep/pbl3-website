Literature Review

Summary

The smart city industry is always developing because of the dynamic nature of today's technology-driven society. In order to meet the demands of this area of innovation, we decided to conduct our study using a web-based application, exploring topics like data gathering, user experience design, and the incorporation of social media interfaces.

To begin, our application relies heavily on geolocation APIs, which provide information vital to the development of smart cities by facilitating the tracking of the precise locations of public amenities like vending machines, trash cans, and restrooms. However, there are several difficulties involved with gathering data using APIs. There are a variety of potential threats to the quality and integrity of the data we collect, including API providers' unwillingness to disclose their data and call limit restrictions. Therefore, it is crucial to study and develop solutions, such as data anonymization, which can lessen the risk of missing data. We acknowledge, however, that in practice, full anonymization of data may not be achievable.

Privacy and security issues must be carefully taken into account when collecting data. The right to privacy is already well-protected by numerous laws, constitutional rights, and legal rulings around the globe. The Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act of 2000 in Canada, the Personal Information Protection Act of 2003 in Japan, the Privacy Act of 1988 in Australia, and the Personal Data Protection Act of 2000 in Argentina are just a few laws protecting individuals' privacy. This also applies to situations when data is gathered by means of application programming interfaces (APIs) made available by social media businesses.

We incorporated a gaming mechanic into the overall design. Gamification tactics have been shown to improve public participation and provide new openings for smart cities to promote sustainable mobility. For instance, Helsinki is testing out a new program called MUV that utilizes game elements to encourage people to alter their commutes and cut down on carbon emissions.

The design also needs to take into account the importance of keeping people interested in our apps. Full-featured, intuitive, and informative apps are more likely to be downloaded and used, according to the aforementioned studies. Therefore, it is important that our programs offer a straightforward interface, a variety of data presentation options, and powerful data management features. To ensure the ongoing improvement of our applications, we also want to keep an eye on emerging technologies like blockchain and AI.

These practical capabilities are generally associated with the growth of Smart Cities. The idea behind "Smart Cities" is to use cutting-edge technologies to boost public services without increasing operating expenses or straining limited municipal resources. By optimizing and improving application features, we hope to make a good contribution to the continuous growth of smart cities, which is why our apps are geared toward providing strong support for the development of smart cities.
