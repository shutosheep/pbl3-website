Effectiveness of Gamification Strategies in Driving User Behavior Change in Smart City Websites and Applications

by Manami Yano

Information technology (IT) has grown rapidly and extensively over the past few years, influencing every facet of our life. Industries that traditionally relied on analog technology, like finance and transportation, have recently embraced IT to improve human support. As a result, IT has evolved into a crucial part of the idea of a "Smart City." Additionally, gamification has captured people's attention and sparked revolutionary transformations in a variety of industries. Given these developments, this literature review will examine the integration of gaming with website and application platforms based on Smart Cities, as well as any potential societal effects.

First, gamification has the potential for encouraging sustainable urban mobility in smart cities. The importance of sustainable urban mobility and the necessity to encourage people to choose environmentally friendly transportation methods are emphasized. (Kazhamiakin, R., & Marconi, A.) The stress life gamification framework, created as part of the EU Project of the same name, is introduced and examined. This framework makes it possible to effortlessly incorporate games into the services and systems that already exist in a smart city. The results of a Rovereto experiment testing the efficiency of gamification in promoting sustainable urban mobility are discussed. These studies highlight how gamification can encourage voluntary behavioral changes and enhance sustainable mobility in urban areas.

Second, gamification's potential to influence changes in energy consumption behavior is also investigated. (AlSkaif, T. 2018) The use of smart meters in accomplishing sustainability objectives is the main focus, and it is necessary to go beyond simple deployment in order to affect residential customers' energy behavior. It is suggested to use an interdisciplinary framework that includes a behavioral model to determine the conditions for behavior modification in residential energy applications. The study looks at how gamification might improve consumer engagement and promote behavior change in the energy sector. (Latifi, G. R., Monfared, M. P., & Khojasteh, H. A. 2020) It also proposes a technological system architecture for energy applications. The projected value streams for various energy market stakeholders are identified, and pertinent game design components are presented. The potential of the suggested framework in several domestic energy applications is examined, emphasizing the function of gamification in encouraging sustainable energy use.

Third, the link between gamification and customer loyalty in the banking industry is another topic of focus. (Malarout, N., & Jain, M. 2020) The review emphasizes the beneficial connection between gamification features in applications and motivation. It looks at studies done in several fields, showing that carefully thought-out and designed gamified applications produce fruitful results. It highlighted how gamification tactics have the ability to increase customer engagement and loyalty by demonstrating the link between gamification and customer loyalty in cooperative banks. However, further empirical research is required to establish the statistical significance of these correlations.

In conclusion, this literature analysis demonstrates how effectively gamification tactics may be incorporated into websites and applications for smart cities to influence user behavior. (Nacke, L. E., & Edwards, E. A. 2017) With regard to promoting sustainable urban transportation, changing energy consumption patterns, and boosting customer loyalty in the banking industry, the incorporation of gamification components within the context of smart cities has shown promising results. Gamification has the ability to enhance the development of smarter, more sustainable cities by utilizing game design features, engaging people, and inspiring voluntary changes in behavior. Gamification may provide value and potential to encourage sustainable habits while raising user engagement and satisfaction levels in the design and implementation of websites and applications for smart cities. Thus, we expect a higher level of appeal and user engagement by incorporating aspects like a roulette game to enhance decisionmaking within our application. Striking a balance is essential to prevent the app from losing its purpose as a smart city tool and from becoming overly game focused. As a result, we will give gamification a secondary role while focusing instead on creating a straightforward program with a distinct objective.
