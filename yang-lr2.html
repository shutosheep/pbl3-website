The impact and application of gamification to smart cities

by Yang Minxu

As global smart city practices increasingly accelerate, cities are challenged in their ability to cope with resource shortages and track global trends. However, a widely researched and explored approach, known as gamification strategies, has shown great potential in promoting sustainable mobility within smart cities. In various studies, gamification systems have been shown to increase public engagement and shape their travel habits, making the public more aware and engaged in the process of sustainable mobility.

A growing number of cities are exploring the possibilities of applying gamification strategies in public policy and urban planning. The city of Helsinki, for example, has been experimenting with the MUV (Mobility Urban Values) project. [1]The project uses so-called 'gamification' to inspire behavioural change within the community, i.e. a game that combines digital and physical experiences to change citizens' habits and promote a shift towards more sustainable and healthier mobility choices, rather than promoting sustainable mobility through costly and rapidly ageing urban infrastructure. improvements. By linking the public's daily travel behaviour to a points system, citizens are incentivised to choose more environmentally friendly travel options such as public transport, cycling or walking. Points can be redeemed at businesses or municipal facilities, thus creating a positive feedback loop.

However, the successful implementation of a gamification strategy requires fine-tuned design and evaluation. Effective incentives require that the reward mechanism matches the needs and interests of the public. In addition, for continuous optimisation, there needs to be an effective way to measure the effectiveness of the gamification strategy and provide feedback. At the same time, the gamified public engagement model brings new challenges. Firstly, data security and privacy issues are key issues that must be addressed, and smart cities must ensure that all data collected and used complies with relevant data protection regulations. Also, how to avoid social inequalities resulting from this model is an issue that needs to be addressed to ensure that all communities and populations can participate and benefit equitably.

Despite these challenges, the role of gamification in terms of sustainable mobility within smart cities is clear. Not only can it be effective in changing citizens' travel habits and making them more aware of and involved in the process of sustainable mobility, but it can also be effective in collecting data about travel and providing valuable information to inform urban planning.
