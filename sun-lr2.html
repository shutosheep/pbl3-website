"Research Methods in Human-Computer Interaction" is an important book, co-authored by J. Lazar, J.H. Feng, and H. Hochheiser. The book provides a broad and in-depth guide to the field of human-computer interaction research, covering all aspects of research methods and techniques.

The core goal of this book is to help readers fully understand and apply the methodology of human-computer interaction research. It provides a systematic methodology to guide researchers and designers to apply effective research techniques in their projects. Through a clear chapter structure and concrete practical examples, the book provides readers with practical guidelines and tools for the evaluation and improvement of human-computer interaction systems.

Research Methods in Human-Computer Interaction covers a wide range of research design and data collection techniques, including user surveys, experimental design, evaluation methods, and data analysis. It not only presents the fundamentals of these methods, but also emphasizes their application and implementation in real projects. In addition, ethical issues, research communication, and the importance of reporting are discussed, enabling readers to gain a comprehensive understanding of the entire research process.

This book is not only suitable for professionals in the field of human-computer interaction research, but also very valuable for students and beginners. It provides readers with a solid foundation to acquire the necessary skills and knowledge in research design and data collection. At the same time, the book also provides a wealth of references for readers to further explore the relevant literature in the field of human-computer interaction research.

Overall, "Research Methods in Human-Computer Interaction" is a detailed and practical guide that provides an important methodological basis for human-computer interaction research. By reading and applying the content, readers will be able to better understand and practice human-computer interaction design, and contribute to creating a better user experience.

References:

Lazar, J., Feng, J. H., & Hochheiser, H. (2017). Research Methods in Human-Computer Interaction. Morgan Kaufmann.
